Biodiversity Net Gain Register - Alpha

Interaction design for a new Biodiversity Net Gain Register, advancing nature's recovery by supporting housing development and increasing habitats across England by 10%.

The challenge

The Environment Act (2021) requires developers to deliver a 'biodiversity net gain' as a condition of their planning permission. To deliver biodiversity gains, habitats can be enhanced on the developers own building site, or at an off-site location, by paying a landowner to manage their land for nature recovery.

The problem we tried to solve; How do we collect data about off-site biodiversity gains and enable Government and local planning authorities to check that they are legitimate, accountable, and transparent?

What we did

Working as a multi-discplinary team, we used design thinking methodolgy to help understand and solve the problems.

Identified riskiest assumptions

We began the project by identifying our riskiest assumptions, giving the team focus.

Mapped out 'as-is' journeys

To understand how biodiversity net gain was currently working, I mapped out our users current journey.

To create the map, I first facilitated workshops involving the business, policy and subject matter experts.

We then validated and iterated the map with several rounds of user research. This gave us a good understanding of our users, uncovered pain points and identified opportunities.

Team brainstorm

I facilitated design workshops involving the entire team, creating a shared understanding of the problem and generating lots of ideas for solving the problem.

Learning by prototyping

I built lots of HTML prototypes to test our ideas with users.

I kept track of all our design hypotheses and research findings in a design decisions log. This proved extremely useful when preparing for our GDS assessment.

Result

We passed our GDS Alpha service assessment in March 2022.
